THE ESSENTIAL ELEMENTS






ELEMENTS


1.Oxygen

To summarize this element, Oxygen is an element
in the periodic table that is needed and used in our day to day lives.
Oxygen is necessary for sustaining human/other organisms life without
Oxygen we would not be able to live, it is very crucial for us to .
It has the chemical symbol "O", an atomic number of 8, and an atomic mass of 16.

2.Hydrogen

Hydrogen is another element that is important to our lives.
It is the most abundant element in the whole universe,
it is found in our sun and many other stars.
Hydrogen is also important due to the fact that it is a major component in water, and we use water daily,
like when we shower, drink, and etc.
Hydrogen has the chemical symbol "H", Hydrogens atomic number is
1 and it has an atomic mass of 1. It is also the lightest element found in the periodic table.



___________________________________________________________________________________________



3.Calcium

Calcium is an element which is important to the human body.
Calcium is important for bone and teeth development especially for babies.
Babies need breastmilk (which has calcium) have good development.
Calcium makes up most of our bones and is a good mineral for us humans to consume.
Calcium has an atomic number of 20 and it's chemical symbol is "Ca" It has an atomic mass of 40.



___________________________________________________________________________________________



4.Nitrogen

Nitrogen is an element that makes up 78% of the atmosphere on earth.
Nitrogen is used by many plants for photosynthesis,
photosynthesis is what plants do to produce Oxygen on earth.
If there was no Nitrogen in our atmosphere many living organisms and plants would die. Nitrogen atomic number is 7
and has a chemical symbol of "N", and Nitrogens atomic mass is 14.

‎5.Carbon

Carbon plays a major role in living organisms. All humans and organisms are Carbon-based which means we have a Carbon-based structure and system. Carbon is also known as the "Chemical Backbone Of life on Earth". Moreover, Carbon compounds regulates Earth's temperature. Carbon has an atomic mass of 12 and an atomic number of 6, Carbon goes by the chemical symbol "C".

COMPOUNDS




1.Water(H₂O)

Water is the most essential compound and it is required for us humans and other animals to live.
Water consists of two Hydrogen atoms and one Oxygen atom.
We use water every single day to shower, to drink, to clean things, and etc.
Water is also one of the most if not the most abundant compound on Earth, infact water makes up 75% of the Earth.
Water can also be found inside our bodies which carry nutrients and oxygen to our cells, around 60% of our body is water.



___________________________________________________________________________________________



2.Glucose(C₆H₁₂O₆)

Glucose is a compound that is essential for our body.
It is what our bodies use as our main energy.
Glucose is a type of sugar that comes from carbohydrates that is in certain food sources we eat, when not needed it is stored in the liver and our muscles.
In short Glucose is what we use for our bodies main energy.



___________________________________________________________________________________________



3.Ethyl Alcohol/Ethanol(C₂H₅OH)

Ethyl Alchohol or Ethanol is used for Disenfectants, solvents, or in some other cases beverages.
It can be used for cleanliness in our everyday lives.
It is widely used as solvents.



___________________________________________________________________________________________



4.Carbon Dioxide(CO₂)

Carbon Dioxide plays a huge role to life on Earth.
We need Carbon Dioxide to sustain life on Earth.
Moreover, plants use carbon Dioxide to create oxygen through photosynthesis.
Carbon dioxide is a colorless and non-flammable gas at normal temperature and pressure.



___________________________________________________________________________________________



5.Sodium Chloride(NaCl)

Sodium Chloride or commonly known as table salt has a wide array of uses in our daily lives.
For instance, salt can be used to preserve foods, provide for our health and nutrition, and etc.
Salt or Sodium Chloride is an essential component to the human diet.
